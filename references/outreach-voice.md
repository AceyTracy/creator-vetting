# Outreach Voice — first-touch emails

The draft the skill produces is a starting point a human edits and sends. It still has to be good enough to send.

## Shape

- Under 120 words. Subject under 6 words, specific, no clickbait.
- Structure: one line proving we watched their content (specific + recent, with the video named) → one line on why Blotato fits their audience (their words, not ours) → concrete offer shape (integration + affiliate, rate anchor from rubric math) → one clear ask (15-min call OR reply with rate card — never both).
- Sign-off: first name only. No corporate signature block.

## Voice

Peer-to-peer, operator-to-operator. We are a creator-built product reaching out to creators — not a brand blasting a media kit. Numbers over adjectives. Short sentences. It should read like it was written by one specific person who watched one specific channel.

## Banned phrases (QA hook rejects these)

"I hope this email finds you well" · "I came across your channel" · "huge fan of your work" · "synergy" · "leverage" · "collab" (as a noun) · "your niche" · "we'd love to partner" (without a concrete offer attached) · "circle back" · "touch base" · any sentence that could be pasted to a different creator unchanged.

## Rate anchoring

Include the CPM-method anchor naturally: "Based on your recent averages we'd budget around $X for a tutorial-native integration, plus 20% recurring affiliate." Anchoring first, with our number, from public math — polite, defensible, and it frames the negotiation.
